The Top 10 Institutes for JEE-Main in India

  • June 10, 2022June 22, 2022

Tracking down the best institutes in India for the JEE-Main entrance exam can be time-consuming and frustrating, but weve done the hard work of narrowing it down to 10 of the best institutes in India. Whether youre looking for one of the top government schools in India or some of the leading private schools and coaching centers, these are some of the best institutes to help you prepare for your JEE-Main entrance exam.

1) IIT Bombay

IIT Bombay is one of Indias premiere institutes, offering a high-quality education to students who are interested in engineering, technology and science. According to Forbes, IIT Bombay is ranked sixth among universities worldwide; it also was awarded a gold rating by QS World University Rankings. IIT Bombay offers many excellent opportunities to students and faculty alike. In addition to its small size there are just over 3,000 undergraduate students and 400 graduate students at IIT Bombay it has a strong culture of student research that puts undergraduates right alongside graduate students and professors on research projects.

2) IIT Delhi

The Institute of Technology at Delhi, colloquially known as IIT Delhi, is one of the best institutes of technology offering undergraduate and postgraduate courses in engineering and science. The institute is headquartered at Hauz Khas Campus and covers an area of over 545 acres with a 120 acre biological park. The campus was ranked 28th among engineering colleges by Outlook India magazine. It offers various courses in eight main departments, namely civil engineering, computer science and engineering, electrical engineering, electronic and communication engineering, materials science and metallurgical engineering, mechanical engineering, chemical engineering and bioengineering.

3) IIT Kanpur

The Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (IITK) is one of the eight new IITs, which were established after a proposal from former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh to then HRD Minister Kapil Sibal. The institution is currently affiliated with UPTU (University of Petroleum and Technology, UP) until it becomes independent. In its initial years, IITK planned to focus on interdisciplinary research and was set up with department wise specializations instead of research labs. It has 26 undergraduate courses leading to BTech degrees along with six MTech programmes. Facilities include state-of-the-art lecture theatres, academic and guest houses, hostels and student canteens among others.

4) IIT Kharagpur

IIT Kharagpur is one of many engineering colleges that are administered by an IIT. Its campus is spread across 2,000 acres and it’s currently ranked among some of India’s best universities. It offers Bachelors, Masters and Doctoral degrees in various fields and it has been consistently ranked as one of India’s top institutions for research, education and employment. There are nearly 3,000 faculty members teaching over 12,000 students at any given time. As far as facilities go, IIT Kharagpur boasts a library containing more than 1 million books making it one of Asias biggest collections along with computer labs with internet access.

5) BITS Pilani Goa Campus

BITS Pilani Goa Campus is one of Indias oldest and most respected educational institutions. In recent years, it has also become popular among foreign students looking to pursue education abroad. The campus has a rich heritage, with many notable figures associated with it including freedom fighter Gopal Krishna Gokhale, who advocated British education for Indians; industrialist Dhirubhai Ambani, who made it possible for local students to attend BITS with financial aid; and Mani Bhavanrao Pant Pratinidhi, a member of Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad IIIs governing council.

6) Vellore Institute of Technology

Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT) is a private deemed university located in Vellore, Tamil Nadu, India. Established in 1984 as Vellore Engineering College by V. Srinivasan, chairman of The Lakshmi Chandrashekharan Group (Lakshmi Entities), which owns and operates educational institutions across several states. It was renamed as VIT University in 2001. In 2010, VIT was granted deemed university status by the University Grants Commission (UGC). Over 6500 students are enrolled at present. The student community includes undergraduates and postgraduates from across 92 districts of Tamil Nadu and other parts of India who live on campus or off campus local area hostels.

7) Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore

IISc is a premiere engineering institute located in Bangalore. It is often regarded as one of Indias best institutes. For engineering, its been ranked among Asias top three best universities. In fact, according to QS World University Rankings 2014/15, IISc ranks second only to Nanyang Technological University (NTU) Singapore among Asian universities that focus on engineering and technology. The research conducted at IISc has been continuously rated among Asias top five over several years by Shanghai Ranking’s Global Ranking of Academic Subjects by subject specialists and scholars. The colleges curriculum includes topics such as experimental laboratories and high quality academic programs.

8) Madras Institute of Technology, Chennai

The Madras Institute of Technology is one of Indias most prestigious institutes. It was founded in 1873 by American missionaries and at first only accepted high school graduates as students, but later changed its admission policy to require that all students have a degree before they can attend. This means that you will graduate with a bachelors degree, which is excellent preparation for applying to a masters program after college if you want to continue your education without having to retake any courses. The institution offers about 40 programs in engineering, technology, architecture and management fields and over 6,000 students attend classes each year. Most of Mtech’s enrollment comes from Tamil Nadu state, but some international students also attend from countries such as Sri Lanka and Nepal.

9) National Institute of Technology, Calicut (NITC)

Located at Malappuram district of Kerala, NITC was established in 1960 and is recognized as an Institute of National Importance by AICTE. The institute admits undergraduates through JEE Advanced while MTech admissions are done through GATE. Over 80% of its 16,000 alumni have made a mark as successful entrepreneurs and research professionals. NITC is a premier institute that has contributed towards development in various fields like IT, Electronics and Communication etc.

10) College of Engineering, Pune (COEP), Pune

COEP is a government college and one of the best institutes for engineering in Pune. It provides a high quality education and has been listed among some of countrys best institutes. In 2014, it was ranked 17th among technical institutions by Outlook Magazine. Admission to COEP is through an engineering entrance exam known as JEE MAIN which is conducted by CBSE . COEP attracts most of its students from western Maharashtra and other parts of India.
